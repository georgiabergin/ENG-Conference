Meet the Panelists

Ezra Klein

Ezra Klein is the founder and editor-at-large of Vox, an American news and opinion website. The website is well known for its explanatory journalism, often in the form of viral videos and articles. The site’s mission is to “move people from curiosity to understanding.” Previously, Ezra has worked as a blogger and columnist for The Washington Post, as an associate editor of The American Prospect, and also as a contributor to both Bloomberg News and MSNBC.

Jill Abramson

Jill Abramson is an American author and journalist. She is the former executive editor of The New York Times, and was the paper’s first female executive editor. Previously, she worked for The Wall Street Journal as an investigative reporter, as well as a deputy bureau chief. She has been a political columnist for the Guardian US since 2016. Abramson ranked fifth on Forbes’ 2012 list of most powerful people and was also named as one of the 500 most powerful people in the world by Foreign Policy.

Laura Poitras

Laura Poitras is an American documentary director and producer. She has received many awards for her work, including a 2015 Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature for her film Citizenfour (about Edward Snowden). Her Film My Country, My Country received a nomination in this category in 2007. She is also known for her national security reporting, having won a 2013 George Polk Award for this. Her reporting on the matter, along side a few other journalists, contributed to The Guardian and The Washington Post winning a joint Pulitzer Prize for Public Service in 2014.

Andrew Blum

Andrew Blum is an American author and journalist who writes about architecture, design, technology, urbanism, art and travel. He has been published in several major magazines including Wired, Newsweek, The Wall Street Journal, the New York Times, the New Yorker, Vanity Fair, BusinessWeek, Slate and Popular Science. He recently wrote a book called “Tubes” which his website describes as “A narrative tour behind the scenes of our everyday lives to see the heart of the Internet itself.”

Rebecca Solnit

Rebecca Solnit is an American writer. She writes about many subjects, including the environment, politics, place and art. She is a contributing editor at Harper’s Magazine, the oldest general-interest monthly in America. The magazine takes a long-form narrative approach to talking about issues going on in the country. Solnit writes for the magazine’s essay “Easy Chair” which she contributes to bi-monthly.

Ethan Zuckerman

Ethan Zuckerman is an American media scholar, blogger and Internet activist. He is the director of MIT’s Center for Civic Media, an Associate Professor of the Practice in Media Arts and Sciences at the school and the author of the book Rewire: Digital Cosmopolitans in the Age of Connection. His research mostly focuses on using media as a tool for social change, the role of technology in international development, and the use of new media technologies by activists.

Jeffrey Goldberg

Jeffrey Goldberg is an American journalist and the current editor-in-chief of The Atlantic. He worked for the magazine for nine years before becoming editor and was known for his coverage of foreign affairs. He’s won many awards and has written eleven cover stories for the magazine.

Kristy Tillman

Kristy Tillman is an experienced designer and the current head of communication at Slack. She pursued design in a Ph.D. program and eventually went back for a second bachelor’s in design. She has worked for Converse, Payless, Reebok and IDEO. Later, she worked for the Society of Grownups, a start-up where she learned about management. Now at Slack, she is building a communication design team.
